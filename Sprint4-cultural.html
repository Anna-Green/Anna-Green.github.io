Neuroplasticity


Noun. "The ability of the brain to form and reorganize synaptic connections,
especially in response to learning or experience or following injury."

When people understand the theory of neuroplasticity they understand that essentially
their learning and knowledge comes down to them and the work that they put into their learning.
This means that anyone is fully capable of handling their knowledge and learning processes.

A way in which this will benefit me is knowing that I can learn anything I want to as long as I reinforce those ideas and keep practicing and memorizing.
I know that I can take on any form of knowledge which also helps me mentally as it gives me the confidence and the ‘not yet’ attitude.

Growth Mindset


Growth mindset deals with our beliefs, resilience and confidence. Essentially it means that our brain can get stronger and smarter with new and challenging learning.
There are two general mindsets when thinking about one’s character. The fixed mindset and the Growth mindset.

The Fixed Mindset


The fixed mindset is exactly as it sounds. A person believes that they were born to have certain traits, born to be smart up until a point
and that all their qualities are carved in stone and there is nothing that they can do about it.


The Growth Mindset


A growth mindset is the complete opposite. It is the belief that the more challenges you face, the smarter you are becoming.
That all of your qualities, attributes and skills can be improved, and more learning can always occur.


So what does this all mean?


The brain is a muscle which means the more you challenge it and use it, the stronger and stronger it becomes.
Scientists have found that a growth mindset creates a passion and drive for learning and education, the process of it,
rather than the need for approval and grades being the be all and end all.


My learning journey


Researching Growth mindset was interesting because it was something I had heard of before, but I had not really looked into it.
I am both astounded that we had not learned about this during school as I feel it would’ve helped a lot of students with their confidence and would change their approach and feelings towards education in general.
