Learning Journey Reflection


What surprised me most about learning core was that I never knew how integral it was to technology sector.
I learned about ‘how’ I learned and how to learn ‘better’ which was interesting.

I really enjoyed the neuroplasticity section, as well as the emotional intelligence and listening and looping portion.
I never thought about soft skills before but I’m glad that I was encouraged to learn them as I think I know more
about myself and my learning style

3 things that I have learned about myself is that I am smarter and more capable than I think, I work well when I have organised
my time and I need to stop comparing myself and my journey to others.

The roles of values, empathy and self-awareness in learning and programming are you need to know your strengths and weaknesses
when it comes to programming and if you need help, ask for it. You need empathy to help others.

The thing that ha surprised me most about core learning was that I really needed it to function as a programmer.
The most challenging aspects were to take a look at myself from an outsider’s point of view and to critique my learning
from the past and ‘how’ I learn. What are the best environments for me to learn in and am I more of a visual learner or critical learner?
I think that we are spending so much time on core learning is because the course is so technically dense that we have to also take care of our wellbeing too.
The time that I have spent learning about core skills has been invaluable. I learned more about myself, my learning style, taking care of myself when
coding and I think that is something that we all needed to learn.


Bootcamp Relflections

What Is My Long Term Goal or Career Pathway?


My hope is that I can get a job as a front end or back end web developer with the beginnings in creating own apps.
I would eventually like to get into AI and Augmented reality too. I want to be in this industry because there are so many
working options (company, from home, freelancer..) and there are so many different pathways that you can take.

What will be my greatest strengths and limitations in bootcamp


I think my greatest strengths will be collaborating and working with others.
Being able to be in constant contact with the tutors is really handy as well.
Some limitations for me personally is grasping all of javascript and something that Im focusing on

Biggest Non Technical challenge


I think keeping the "not yet" mentality in mind is important for me. To remember that Imn still new at this
and too not put unnessecary pressure on myself.

What non technical skills would you like to see developed?


I would like to get more confident in speaking infront of groups and giving and recieving feedback.

What are my expectations of myself during bootcamp?


My expectations of myself are that if I need help, I can ask my peers or tutors. I will keep
my workload to set hours so that I have the time to rest if I need to. Its also important to
take small breaks, do some meditation and to do enough work so that at the end of the day, I can
feel good about my day.
