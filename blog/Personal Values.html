I do agree that some of these are me but some also aren’t. I am Maori and living and growing up in New Zealand has taught me a lot about culture and identity.
This has helped me form my own values and somethings I consider very important are family, honesty, kindness, and fairness.
Kindness can make a world of difference to just one person and I personally believe that its important to be kind whenever possible.

My strengths and limitations in my learning journey: I love to learn new things but if I don’t understand it, I have a hard time gaining that motivation and drive to go back and re-learn it. I can get stressed and give up quite easily so I am trying to build resistance and start a ‘not-yet’ attitude towards my learning. Once I do learn something though, I love to help and teach others who are struggling so that I can be a resource that they can depend on. When I am focused, I love the energy of learning new skills that I can adapt to other parts of my life.

One of my strengths that I am learning to develop is the ‘not-yet’ mindset and I think that that can help towards any obstacles that I may encounter both in this journey and in other parts of my life. Just knowing that I need to give myself time in order to know what I need and to not compare myself to others. I have worked with groups of others before who were all expecting different things out of a project so I think honesty and keeping clear about the expectations of the outcomes of said project is super important. Open communication is necessary for success.
